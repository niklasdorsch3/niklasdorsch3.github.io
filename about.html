About the Artist

Niklas Dorsch

Niklas Dorsch began painting in 2019 after moving to New York, where classes at the New York Studio School deepened his commitment to art. Transitioning from drawing to painting allowed him to explore a more intuitive, emotional process. His work focuses on abstract landscapes inspired by mythical narratives—especially German fairytales—where organic forms flow, clash, and shape space in unexpected ways.

Using wet-on-wet techniques and a minimal brush palette, Niklas begins with abstract backgrounds and gradually "carves out" imagined forms. Music often guides the rhythm and structure of each composition. He hopes his paintings evoke curiosity and a quiet sense of loneliness—an emotional thread he believes speaks to the human condition.

Influences include Egon Schiele's expressive distortions, Edward Hopper's atmospheric solitude, and Pablo Picasso's spatial experimentation. Looking ahead, Niklas plans to expand his practice through an artist residency or dedicated studio space, deepening his exploration of mood and myth through paint.
